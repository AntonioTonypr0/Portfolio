History

The history of tacos goes back as far as the 18th century. They were most likely created by Mexican miners. The reason for that is that the word "taco" reffered to the little charges they would use to excavate the ore. A taco is a traditional Mexican food. It consists of a small hand-sized corn or wheat based tortilla filled with meat and anything you want to add inside of it, people normally prepares tacos with cilantro cebolla and salsa.

Making this recipe is a effortless, meat is easy to cut, and tortillas are easy to heat up.

Ingredients for 3 adults

  • 1 1/2 pound of arrachera, cleaned and cut at the supermarket
  • 2 packs of corn tortillas (best flavor in town from el Milagro)
  • 1 cup of mojo of Goya
  • Green Lime

Directions

  1. Go to the supermarket and buy the ingredients.
  2. Get back home and go to the kitchen.
  3. Take out all your groceries.
  4. Take out a knife, pot, tongs, comal, and a cutting board.
  5. Cut the meat into small chunks and marinate it with mojo Goya and let it rest for 1-5 hours depending how hungry you are
  6. Take out the pan and heat it up, add oil and throw in the meat
  7. Move the meat every 15-30 seconds with your tongs
  8. When cooked for a good amount and not raw, take out the meat from the fire
  9. Take out the comal and heat the tortillas well
  10. Put the meat in the tortillas and add lime and a hot sauce if you would like
  11. Eat Up!
